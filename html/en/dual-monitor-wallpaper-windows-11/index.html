Why dual-monitor wallpapers go wrong

Two screens usually do not share one ideal rectangle.

One display may be taller, offset, or vertical.

Standard 'Fill' or 'Span' modes often lead to frustrating results on uneven screens.

  • Different heights.
  • Different aspect ratios.
  • Focal point landing on a bezel.

How WallCraft solves this

WallCraft keeps your images visible instead of forcing a panoramic crop.

It treats each screen as a unique frame within the composition.
